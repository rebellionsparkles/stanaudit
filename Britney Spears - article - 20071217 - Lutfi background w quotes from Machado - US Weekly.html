Headnote
As Britney's loved ones worry about Sam Lutfi's hold on her, Us uncovers the truth about his mysterious past
UNTIL SHE VANISHED for a half hour, Britney Spears was the life of the Scandinavian Style Mansion bash hosted
December 1 in a private Los Angeles home. Under the ever-watchful eye of her constant companion Sam Lutfi,
Spears mugged for the cameras as her BFF AlIi Sims sampled the gift suite, grabbing them $30,000 worth of furs
and sunglasses. But just after the songstress blew out the candles on the birthday cake brought in to toast her
26th, she slipped away to a bathroom and bawled her brown eyes out. "I'm so depressed," a source overhead her
say when she emerged 30 minutes later. "They're all so sweet getting me the cake and singing. But my family isn't
here. It's been so hard. I'm just so depressed."
But duty called, so she grabbed a Belvedere Vodka and tonic and went downstairs. Lutfi had work for her: Her
former gal pal Paris Hilton had come to the party, and the mysterious Mr. Lutfi wanted to be sure Spears was
photographed with her. "Sam was orchestrating everything," a party insider tells Us. "He was very much in charge."
Since late summer, when he seemed to appear from nowhere, Lutfi, 33 (also known as Osamah Sam Lutfi, among
other aliases), who has been described as a film producer, has become an integral part of Spears' life. His role as
her de facto manager and publicist has ranged from denouncing on Ryan Seacrest's L.A. radio show In Touch's
ridiculous pregnancy story to helping her organize her music-video shoots and accompanying her on outings with
her boys, Sean, 2, and Jayden, 15 months. (Lutfi even stays overnight at her L.A.-area homes, but they remain
platonic.) But Us has uncovered court documents - including two restraining-order applications detailing his
alleged violent tendencies - that only add to the concern shared by Spears' loved ones that Lutfi has inserted
himself into her life under false pretenses. "They are frightened," a source close to Spears' parents, Jamie and
Lynne, tells Us of the relationship (which has come to resemble that of Howard K. Stern and the late Anna Nicole
Smith): "They know she is being taken advantage of." Seconds another Spears pal, "He uses her to be in her world,
and she uses him to do everything. But he's in control."

But perhaps not of his own temper. "He's a nightmare," says Jumana Issa, who was granted a temporary
restraining order in 2005 after she accused Lutfi - a business acquaintance - of harassing her. According to her
complaint filed in Santa Monica, California: "Mr. Lutfi has harassed me repeatedly with obscene e-mails, offensive
faxes, telephone voice mails (around 1 A.M. to 3 A.M.), outof-control and outrageous telephone hang-ups (around
15 to 30 a day), along witb offensive . . . comments." A memorable fax? "Peel your-self away from all the candy
your dumb attitude and overhangin [sic] belly and answei my e-mails.... Put the donuts [sic] down and call me."
Issa's account is eerily similar to that of Lutfi's neighbor Douglas Snoland. According to his complaint, on
September 6, 2004, Lutfi begar pounding on the door of the L.A. home Snoland, then 41 shared with his then-73-
year-old disabled mother after Lutfi suspected Snoland had his Honda Civic towed. After pounding on kitchen
windows and damaging the front door when he tried to kick it down Snoland said Lutfi followed up with constant
late-night hangups. In the complaint, Snoland accused Lutfi of saying: "I will beat your ass.... Your mother is a f-
king old hag. You an a f-got. You will regret the day you ever met me." Lutfi's attorney denied the threats but a
judge granted a three year restraining order to Lutfi to stay at least 15 feet away from Snoland and his mom.
Lutfi's short fuse was 01 display August 27 when Aaron Cohen, a member of security team hired to served Lutfi
with a subpoena to be deposed in Spears' custody battle with ex-husband Kevin Federline, pulled up alongside him.
To avoid being served Lutfi "crashed through two our cars to get away," Cohen a former Israeli commande tells Us.
"He's the most elusive witness I've ever come across. Lutfi was later served, but did not show up for his November
30 deposition, which was rescheduled for January. Say Federline's attorney, Mark Vincent Kaplan, "A judge could
order a warrant" if he skips that session.
How He Got to Her
So exactly how did Spears get involved with someone like this? According to a source, Spears met Lutfi at a
William Morris Agency party in L.A. earlier this year. "He then started visiting her house and calling her. At first he
said his name was Jeff, but then said it was Sam." But one of Spears' ex-assistants, Kalie Machado, says that
wasn't the first time Lutfi passed himself off as someone he wasn't. About two weeks after Spears' February 21
check-in to Promises Treatment Center in Malibu, California, Machado remembers that Lutfi called and told her "he
was a private eye for Kevin and that Kevin had screwed him, so he was willing to hand over all this confidential
information on Britney." (Federline's rep vehemently denied any connection.)
Lutfi insisted they meet, says Machado, who reluctantly agreed to see him at a Santa Monica, California,
Starbucks. "He started telling me all these things," she says. "He said there was a warrant to search [Spears']
Malibu house for drugs, and I know there's a tap on your phone, and I know you're being followed.'" But for
someone who claimed to know so much, says Machado, "he was asking weird questions, like, 'What do you guys
do during the day?' 'Where do you go?' I was like, 'What a freak,' so I just ended up leaving." Fast-forward to late
summer, Machado says, "and I see him with Britney all the time! I think he's some kind of stalker. He's psycho."
According to another source, someone whom they believe to be Lutfi made a similarly bizarre call on July 2. "He
said the police were going to search her house for drugs. It was weird. There was no reason for the police to alert
someone who barely knew Britney that they were going to search her house. It made people extremely suspicious."
Several people close to Spears suspect that Lutfi made these calls in an effort to manipulate Spears into trusting
him.
But in the end, Lutfi's infiltration of her camp was simple, says another Spears confidant. "She's so desperate for a

Fact or Fiction?
On the Internet Movie Database Web site (imdb.com), where the entertainment industry posts credits, Lutfi is listed
as a producer of the 1998 B-movie Bug Buster. The director - and actual producer - Lorenzo Doumani tells Us that's
not so. "He was my assistant," says Doumani, adding that Lutfi was paid $350 a week and had been working as a
busboy. "He was a hustler type, a fast-talking kid."
Though it's not clear that Lutfi padded his résumé (the database is monitored by the site's managers), another
credit, as an associate producer of the 2001 short Crossing Cords (executive-produced by Ben Affleck), may also
be incorrect. Says one of its stars, "I have never heard of him in my life."
Good for Business
His career path may be confusing, but a source familiar with Spears' dealings with her music label, Jive Records,
tells Us that Lutfi has his good points professionally, and that the November 27 video shoot for her follow-up
"Piece of Me" would not have happened without him: "He is the only one that can get Britney to do anything."
But her parents aren't convinced. One Spears source says, "She's so vulnerable. But there's nothing they can do.
They are powerless."

THE SAM LUTFI FILE
Oh, what a tangled web! Maybe Spears should have run a background check on her go-to guy.
His aliases Among the names associated with Spears' pal are Osamah Sam Lutfi, Osama N. Lutti and Osamah N.
Lutfi.
Loan trouble In 2004, he was sued by Southwest Student Services for nonpayment of student loans in the amount
of $12,328.85 (just one of at least six civil suits brought against Lutfi), which stemmed from his enrollment at
University of Southern California in the '90s.
Anger issues In 2004 and 2005, temporary restraining orders were granted against Lutfi after L.A. neighbor
Douglas Snoland and business cohort Jumana Issa filed claims of harassment and threats of bodily harm.

Oops . . . they did it again! One month after Life &Style Weekly said Britney Spears was in a "panic" fearing she was
